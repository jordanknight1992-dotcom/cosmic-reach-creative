## Part 1 — 30-Minute Onboarding Call Script

Use this call to go deeper on the intake form. The form gives you structure; the call gives you nuance, tone, and the unsaid things.

**Before the call:** Read the intake form in full. Note anything vague, contradictory, or worth probing.

---

### Opening (2 min)

> "Thanks for your time. I've read through your intake form and I want to make sure the report reflects what's actually happening — not just what's easy to describe. I'll ask some direct questions. There are no wrong answers."

---

### Section 1 — The Business (5 min)

1. Walk me through what you sell and who you sell it to. Pretend I'm someone who just found your website.
2. What do your best customers have in common — not demographics, but the situation they're in when they find you?
3. What do you say when someone asks "what does your company do?" at a networking event?

---

### Section 2 — The Friction (8 min)

4. Where does marketing feel like it's working — even a little?
5. Where does it feel like effort isn't translating into results?
6. When a lead doesn't convert, what's the most common reason you hear — or guess?
7. Has your messaging changed significantly in the last 12 months? What drove that?
8. Is there a competitor or company whose marketing you genuinely respect? What do they do well?

---

### Section 3 — The Offer (5 min)

9. How do people typically find out about your pricing? Is it visible, or do they have to ask?
10. What's the first step you ask someone to take when they're interested? How often does that actually happen?
11. What objections come up most often before someone buys?

---

### Section 4 — The Systems (5 min)

12. What happens to someone after they fill out a form or show interest — is there an automated follow-up, or is it manual?
13. What tools are you using for email, CRM, or marketing automation?
14. How often does your team publish content or communicate with your audience?

---

### Section 5 — The Numbers (5 min)

15. What metric do you look at most often to gauge marketing performance?
16. Do you know your lead-to-customer conversion rate, even roughly?
17. If I asked your leadership team "is marketing working?", what would they say — and what would they base that on?

---

### Closing (2 min)

18. Is there anything about the business that you think is important context — something that doesn't fit neatly into a form?
19. What would make this report feel genuinely useful to you?

> "That's everything I need. I'll have the report to you within 3–5 business days. It will be direct and specific — not a generic checklist."

## Quality Checklist Before Sending

Before sending the report, verify:

- [ ] Every "Observed Friction" references something specific — a URL, a quote, a form answer, or a call note
- [ ] No section uses generic language that could apply to any company
- [ ] The Implementation Path names a specific sprint and explains why, not just "here are the options"
- [ ] The week-by-week plan is actionable without additional context
- [ ] The report is 1,500–2,000 words minimum
- [ ] The tone is calm and analytical throughout — no hype phrases like "unlock your potential" or "skyrocket growth"
- [ ] The Priority Shift table has exactly 5 rows
- [ ] The "What Improvement Looks Like" section is written in present tense
